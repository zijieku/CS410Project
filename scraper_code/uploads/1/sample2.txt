Big Data Freelance journalist Share to facebook Share to twitter Share to linkedin Google may change its policy on political ads, the Wall Street Journal reported. The news follows Twitters announcement last week that its no longer accepting political and issue ads as of Nov. 22. Facebook, meanwhile, has said it will continue to accept the ads and wont fact-check them, prompting criticism from some on the left and praise from some on the right. With the 2020 presidential election less than a year away, there has been a growing debate over political ads on social media. A Trump campaign ad last month included an unsubstantiated claim on former Vice President Joe Biden's role in the ouster of a Ukrainian prosecutor, the Journal said. Facebook also ran the ad and refused to take it down following complaints by the Biden and Elizabeth Warren campaigns. Its unclear exactly what changes Google is considering, the Journal said, citing people familiar with the matter. The company is expected to tell employees more details this week, the story said.
