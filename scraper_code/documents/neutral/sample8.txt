(Photo by Ali Balikci/Anadolu Agency/Getty Images)Getty ImagesIntel (NASDAQ: INTC) spent 13% of its money on selling, general & administrative (SG&A) expenses in 2018. It has been spending less money on SG&A in the recent years. In fact, Intels SG&A, when looked at as a percentage of its revenue, has declined from 14% in 2016 to <10% in 2018. This can partly be attributed to divesture of its security business, and an overall reduction in marketing programs. The biggest expense item for Intel is its cost of goods sold, which accounted for 53% of total expenses in 2018, followed by research & development (R&D) at 27%. In this note we discuss the key drivers of Intels expenses. Operating Expenses Have Increased From $46.3 Billion In 2016 To $47.5 Billion In 2018, Primarily Driven By $4.0 Billion Increase In COGS, $0.9 Billion Increase In R&D, $1.6 Billion Decline In SG&A, And $1.9 Billion Decline In Other Operating Expenses. COGS Grew From $23.2 Billion In 2016 To $27.1 Billion In 2018, Driven By Sales Growth. As A % Revenues, COGS Has Declined From 39% to 38% Over The Same Period. However, It Is Expected To Be Higher In 2019, Due To Absence of Grants And Lower ASPs For NAND Products. R&D Expenses Have Increased Slightly From $12.7 Billion In 2016 To $13.5 Billion In 2018, Driven By Increased Investments In Data Centric Business And 7nm Technology. As A % of Revenues, R&D Has Declined From 21% To 19% Over The Same Period. SG&A Expenses Declined From $8.4 Billion In 2016 To $6.8 Billion In 2018, Due To Reduction In Marketing Expenses, And Divestiture of Its Security Unit.
